European Aspects of Spatial Development 
 
Autumn Semester 2024 
ETH Course Number 103-0569-00L 
3 ECTS 
 
 
 
 
Description 
 
 
Following the insight into historical perspective and contemporary models of governance and planning, the 
course focuses on the international dimension of spatial planning in Europe. This includes discussing how 
European spatial policy is made and by whom, how planners can participate in such a process and how they 
can address transnational challenges of spatial development cooperatively. 
 
Keeping the general aim of exploring the European dimension of spatial planning in mind, the specific course 
learning objectives are as follows: 
- 
to interpret the history of spatial planning at the transnational scale 
- 
to understand and explain the content of the European spatial policy agenda 
- 
to describe and analyse the role of territorial cooperation in making European spatial development 
patterns and planning procedures 
- 
to discuss the changing role of planners and evaluate the ways of their engagement in European spatial 
policy-making 
 
 
Prerequisites 
 
 
The course is offered in the Spatial Development and Infrastructure Systems, Geomatic Engineering, 
Architecture, and Integrated Engineering Systems Master Programme of ETH Zurich. The course is only for 
master students; otherwise, special permission from the lecturer is required. No specific prior knowledge is 
necessary for attending the course.

Course online 
 
 
 
All necessary documents and information for the lecture will be provided at the Moodle platform, from the following 
link: https://moodle-app2.let.ethz.ch/course/view.php?id=23478 
 
In order to gain access to the course materials, the students need to be enrolled for the course. 
 
 
 
Performance assessment 
 
 
 
Type of performance assessment: session examination 
 
Examination mode: Written exam (90 min) – 80 % of the final grade; blended written exam (combination of 
multiple-choice questions and open-ended questions) 
 
Further information on examination mode: The course semester activities include a continuous performance 
assessment task that does not need to be passed on its own, i.e., it is not obligatory; it is awarded a grade that 
counts proportionally towards the total course unit grade (20%). The task is in the form of a presentation (i.e., 
independent study on topics discussed during lectures). The presentation is scheduled towards the end of the 
semester. 
 
In addition, by participating in the discussion following guest lectures, students may achieve points (max. 5 
points/guest lecture), which will be counted towards up to 15% of the final course grade. 
 
Precise information on the examination mode will be given at the first lecture of the course and provided in 
Moodle.
