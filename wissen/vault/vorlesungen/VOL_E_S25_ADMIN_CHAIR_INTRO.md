VOYAGE PITTORESQUE is a playful 
seminar launched by VOLUPTAS which focuses on the infinite and often hidden 
complexity in our immediate environment. Over the coming years, VOYAGE 
PITTORESQUE will bring us to specific territories and neighbourhoods that are 
closely related to the teaching team of 
VOLUPTAS. Thanks to the precise geographical boundaries and the local connections, VOYAGE PITTORESQUE offers 
time and knowledge to critically engage 
with an environment.
“Berlin is an experiment without hypothesis. Multiple identities enable it to 
absorb the ‘other’. Berlin is ugly, but intense. Its qualities were never intended. 
There is not a single idea, a single concept, or a single geometry which characterizes this city as a whole.” PHILIPP 
OSWALT, BERLIN – CITY WITHOUT FORM 
(2000) 
The product of a multitude of invisible 
forces, Berlin’s urban space lacks any singular identity. The absences in its urban 
fabric, produced through destruction and 
fragmentation, shift the focus of observation from the solid object to that which 
lies in between. Berlin, like few other cities, is defined by its voids – spaces in 
which things resonate. A city of sound.
Shifts in sound, musical performance, 
and auditory perception have always been 
at the forefront of the city’s urban transformation. The city’s more recent development can be traced through the places in 
which new experiences of sound and 
spaces for listening emerged. In Berlin’s 
history, new modes of perception went 
hand in hand with historical change, subversion, and political dissonance. Changes 
in perception lead to changes in society. 
In Berlin, sound creates. 
 On our journey, we will discover spaces 
which expand our reading of Berlin 
through a spectrum of sounds, from ambient noise to high music. Experiencing the 
city through spaces for listening, we will 
discover the ways in which it has been 
shaped, and is still shaped, by sound in all 
its facets: controlled sound and harmonic 
resonance, industrial noise and cultural 
rebirth, environmental ambience and ecological thought. 
 Different practices of listening, sensing, 
and recording will introduce us to some of 
the lesser known dimensions of Berlin’s 
complex and layered reality. Guided by 
specific musical and sonic events, as well 
as the insight of a range of musicians and 
sound artists, we will discover a diverse 
urban fabric through sound. Opening our 
ears will open us up to experience, experiment, and uncertainty. In Berlin’s varied 
textures of noise and silence, we can 
catch a break – and hear things that have 
not yet been put into language. Honing in 
on all parts of our auditory spectrum will 
reveal new qualities and stories, questioning our preconceptions of what this city is 
and what it might become.
