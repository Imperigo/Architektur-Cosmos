# Druck 2

## S. 1

Expanding Traces
To navigate the fine line between an empty 
white space and a proportionally inflated 
reconstruction of Cai’s artworks, we have 
engaged with Cai’s work cycle.
Throughout our study, we encountered four 
foundational concepts that connect our 
experiments with Cai’s art: pressure, layering, 
traces, and proportion.
The next step is to develop the project 
within Cai’s creative cycle. Early on, we were 
captivated by the moment of explosion, which 
he presented in performances before little or 
no audience. Given the nature of gunpowder, 
this spectacle can only be witnessed in the 
here and now. We saw it as our task to capture 
this ephemeral space in a fixed structure, 
making it possible to experience it over time.
Glass serves as the counterpart to gunpowder. 
The fragility and transparency of glass allow 
us to work with three-dimensional layering, 
depicting traces and surface changes 
(particles and smoke) while also representing 
heat and sound waves in the form of cracks and 
fusions.
The Dimensions of the glass panels is derived 
from the origin of the artwork White Tone. 
They are ‘pre-exploded’ and then suspended in 
space.
‘Using Explosives to create Beauty, rather than warfare and 
violence, provides a slither of hope for our shared 
human future.‘
‘Reflecting concern for humanity’s estrangement from nature 
and fascination with unseen cosmic forces‘
