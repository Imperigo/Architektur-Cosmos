# You enter the room2

## S. 1

You Enter the Room. 
First your sense of “smell” kicks in. 
A strong and pervasive Oduor of burnt Materials and sulfur oxide lingers thickly in the 
air. You get a mixture of contradictory feelings, of curiosity and dread, of fondness 
and disgust, of joy and sorrow, of peace and restlessness, of order and chaos.
Your sense of „sight“ brings you back to the room you’re about to visit. The Artwork 
“white Tone” of Cai Guo Qiang is barely visible through all the layers of cracked and 
fogged up glass. On it you see traces of Soot in form of whirls that seem to get more 
intense the deeper you peer into the structure.
You put one foot in front of the other, leaving footprints behind. Like snow, the 
particles of smoke have settled down on the bottom of the room and left a layer of 
sulfur dust on the wooden walkway.
You start to progressively lose yourself in the layers of more and more intensely 
cracked glass. The outside world blurs behind the suspended glass panes 
increasingly coated by soot. The glass is now starting to show traces of immense 
heat such as bubbling and melting on the surface, distorting your surroundings 
further. You look for a point of reference, the only point of reference where every 
glass pane is pointing to, White tone.
The light is starting to fade into a Bright and colorless tone. It has been refracted 
countless times, reflecting equal quantities of all frequencies of visible light.
Standing on the final platform, its materiality closer to charcoal than wood, and 
looking at Cai’s art, the room now feels unlimited, its end not visible and it’s scale not 
really identifiable. You feel yourself as if in cosmic dust. It seems as if there are 
unseen cosmic forces at play, elevating the ambiguous feelings of curiosity and 
dread, of fondness and disgust, of joy and sorrow, of peace and restlessness, of 
construction and destruction, of life and death.
