## S. 2

Space for WHITE TONE by Cai Guo Qiang
Cai Guo-Qiang is a renowned Chinese artist 
celebrated for his unique method of creating 
art through gunpowder explosions and his 
spectacular fireworks displays. His creative 
process often unfolds in a workshop-like 
environment, usually with his team and 
occasionally in front of a small audience. Cai’s 
technique involves carefully layering materials 
such as black powder, rice paper, cardboard, and 
stones onto a substrate—typically canvas, paper, 
glass, or, less commonly, three-dimensional 
forms. Once the setup is complete, the gunpowder 
is ignited, creating an explosion that leaves 
intricate traces on the surface. These marked 
surfaces become the finished artworks.
Glass, a foundational material in Cai’s practice, 
also plays a central role in our project. However, 
we reverse his layering technique to “capture” 
the fleeting moments of the explosion. Instead 
of allowing smoke, particles, and scents to 
dissipate into the air, we suspend a glass 
substrate above the explosion, enabling it to 
collect these ephemeral elements.
The distance between the glass and the 
gunpowder is carefully calibrated, determining 
the patterns that are imprinted. To prevent the 
final work from visually competing with the 
original explosion, the resulting glass wall piece 
is fragmented and rearranged into a new pattern. 
This transformation recreates the essence of an 
explosion within the space, offering an immersive 
experience without directly replicating the 
artwork.
Now, we can step into the impact.
