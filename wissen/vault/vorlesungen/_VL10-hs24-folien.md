Axiomes for modern Landscape (according to Garrett Eckbo)
1.
A denial of historical styles. Instead, landscape expression 
derivers from a rational approach to the conditions created by 
industrial society, the site and the program. 
2.
A concern for space rather than pattern, deriving a model from 
contemporary architecture.
3.
Landscapes are for people
4.
The destruction of the axis
5.
Plants are used for their individual qualities as botanical entities 
and sculpture. 
6.
Integration of house and garden, not ”house-and-then-a-garden.”
