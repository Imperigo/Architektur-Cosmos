According to a now discredited legend, the first modern mechanical clock […] was 
invented by the monk named Gerbert [of Aurillac] near the close of the tenth century 
[…]. The clock is not merely a means of keeping track of the hours, but of 
synchronizing the actions of men […]. The instrument presently spread outside the 
monastery, and the regular striking of the bells brought a new regularity into the life of 
the workman […]. The clock, not the steam-engine, is the key-machine of the modern 
industrial age […]. In its relationship to […] its own special product, accurate timing, 
the clock has been the foremost machine in modern technics.
