Planning: pure political activity of the capitalist state 
 
- Rational planning = positivist approach 
→ science-based decision-making 
e.g. urban growth: result of new technologies and 
population migrations 
- Neo-Marxist planning = structuralist approach 
→ power structure behind the visible phenomena 
e.g. urban growth: result of capitalists’ need for a large, 
cheap, easily controlled labour force and increased 
production 
 
→ Space: a social product 
→ Social production of space: a tool of hegemonic class to 
reproduce its dominance 
 
Planning: a social practice 
Three functions of planning 
1. Rationalisation and legitimization 
2. Negotiation and mediation btw diff. demands of the 
various groups of capital 
3. A regulator of the pressure of the governed classes when 
they are calm and when they protest 
 
→ planner: a mere service of capital 
→ public interest: the one of the class 
A tool of the capital 
- Diminishing the role of planners 
- Highlighting the gap btw theory and practice 
→ Neo-Marxist model in spatial planning 
blocks the political debate 
- No new definitions of planner’s tasks (other 
than to enter the class struggle) 
 
→ Planner: the one to reveal contradiction and 
promote social innovation

- Critical listening

- Dialogue 
- Self-reflectivity of the planner

and various interests
