## S. 43

“The third part of such drawing is the one we mentioned and 
called ‘interior wall,’ with its ornaments. This is no less 
necessary than the other two, and it is made in the same way as 
the ‘exterior wall’ from the plan and parallel lines. It shows half 
of the inside of the building—as if were cut down the middle.”
“In such drawings there is never to be any diminishing at the 
extremities, even if the building were circular, nor even if it were 
rectangular and it was done in order to show two sides. This is 
because an architect cannot pick up correct measurements from a 
foreshortened line. This is necessary for a practice that demands 
perfectly accurate measurements, and is drawn with parallel lines 
rather than ones that appear to be parallel but are not.”
Raphael and Baldassare Castiglione, “Letter to Leo X”, 1519-21, 
Munich, Cod. It. 37b, f. 10 r.
Antonio da Sangallo the Younger, choir of Santa Maria 
Sopra Minerva, 178 A r., Gabinetto Disegni e Stampe degli 
Uffizi, Florence, 1536
