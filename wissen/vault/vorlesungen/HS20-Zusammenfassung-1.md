Relevance of Ideology in Planning: 
- 
All these treatments of ideology in planning literature suggest: 
➢ A common acceptance that ideas, concepts, the ideologies they comprise and the 
discourses through which they find expression matter, 
➢ They have the power to shape the terms by which political and social reality is 
understood, articulated and (re)shaped through planning practice 
- 
But the multiple ways in which the concept of ideology has been used in planning implies 
both its strength and weakness as an analytical tool: 
➢ The concept of ideology in its various forms can be a powerful means to help us 
understand the political nature of planning 
➢ However, echoing Wildavsky’s (1973) famous critique of planning, it also suggests that 
ideology as an analytical category could come perilously close to encompassing 
everything and therefore meaning nothing.
