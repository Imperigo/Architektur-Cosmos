# Reader Emerson_Arenella

## S. 1

Studio Tom Emerson
www.emerson.arch.ethz.ch
Spring Semester 2025
Arenella
The post-industrial area Ex-Chimica in the district of 
Arenella is a microcosm of Palermo’s social and economic 
history of the last century. Founded by German immigrants 
to produce citric acid from lemon trees cultivated in the 
Conca d’Oro region, the factory grew into one of the biggest 
chemical plants in Europe by the middle of the 20th century. 
In the 1960s, the fortunes of the factory declined rapidly 
until it was abandoned in 1965. 
Today, it stands among the many ruins of contemporary 
Palermo: part of the city, but unused except for non-human 
residents. Some believe it is a lost cause, its resurrection a 
romantic dream that should be left to the wrecking ball. 
Other local actors see incredible human, ecological and 
architectural potential lying dormant in this unique situation 
between Monte Pellegrino and the Tyrrhenian sea. On 
invitation by the local organisation Studio Rizoma, we 
will produce a new kind of Atlas, anchored in the social, 
architectural and natural reality of Palermo. The Atlas will 
draw knowledge from the material of the site and local 
partners, including youth associations and shipbuilders, 
to imagine a first step into the future for Arenella and 
Palermo, which mirrors an official call for visions for the 
site. The specific architectural difficulty of saving the site 
for the public good is matched by wider challenges of a 
traumatic past, contemporary migration, and tourism which 
is transforming the city from far beyond its shores. However, 
if migration is the most pressing crisis of the Mediterranean 
today, it has also been the making of Palermo’s exquisitely 
layered history.
 
The studio will involve a trip to Palermo for extensive 
site investigation during an integrated Seminar Week. The 
budget for the latter and the design studio is expected to be 
max. 750 CHF (Costs Category C). Students who are fluent 
in Italian are welcome.
Ex-Chimica Arenella, 2025, Studio Tom Emerson
