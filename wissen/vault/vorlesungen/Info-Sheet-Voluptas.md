PATHFINDER 5  is dedicated to the timeless city of Berlin: its intricate history, morphology, 
mythology… all canonical spatial parameters suggested by Lefebvre: political, sociological, 
anthropological, economical, temporal.* 
Our interest will focus both on the banal and the sublime, on habits and exceptions, 
individual rites and social encounters. As the ordinary interweaves thousands of beats, 
repetitive tunes and syncopated breaks, there can be no territoriality without its 
temporalities, may it be that of urbanisation, of experience, or of dreams. 
Critical considerations, interlaced with the lure of fiction, shall initiate alternate (hi)stories 
and cityscapes. 
In the design studio we will draft, mount, design, discuss, play… confront our perspectives 
onto contemporaneity and ultimately imagine audacious yet playful architectural and 
territorial fictions.
A series of lectures, screenings and discussions will enrich our investigations.
*Lefebvre, Henri. The Production of Space (1974)
& Rhythmanalysis: Space, Time and Everyday Life (1992)
METHODOLOGY 
1. Conduct a survey of Berlin: driven by an agent and by your own desires, draw the chosen 
environment within the city considering its implications and problematics.
2. Depict the experience of the environment through architectural notations.
3. After having identified a problematic, use your imagination and critical thinking, to 
design a potential solution. 
4. Elaborate a coherent narrative supported by a curated selection of multidisciplinary 
sources.

INTERMEDIATE REVIEW Research Phase: Survey & Experience
A. PRESENTATION: linear argument and narrative elaborating all aspects of the 
research and project proposal, specifically:
–City: articulate reading of the city in respect to the research and analysis (history, 
morphology, politics, socio-economics, etc.)
–Agent/Environment: articulate understanding of the Agent/Environment and 
deduction of potent Agencies in respect to the City and given Environment
–Timeframe: articulate Timeframe relating to the investigation and topics of interest
–Statement: short project statement formulated as a ‚promise‘ 
–Arsenal: selection of references (images, plans, filmstills, drawings, orthophotos, 
archive, etc.) relating to all the aformentioned features
B. SURVEY / BOARDS: 
The Survey aims at capturing and mapping out the essence of the field/path: a 
synthesis or reduction.
–Drawing 1: A0 Draft of the field/path within the city (line drawing)
–Image 1: A0 Unprocessed Image capturing the essence of the field/path, according 
to the topics of interest
C. EXPERIENCE / BOARDS: 
The Experience aims at expressing and mapping out the experience of the field/path: 
an augmentation/intensification. 
–Drawing 2: A0 Draft of the augmented/intensified field/path (line drawing) 
–Image 2: A0 Montage of the augmented/intensified experience of/along the field/
path
FINAL REVIEWS Project Phase: DESIGN
A. PRESENTATION 
– Including Agent, Timeframe, City, Statement
– Update according to project development and critique
– Arsenal: Extended/Updated selection of references (images, plans, filmstills, 
drawings, orthophotos, archive, etc.) relating to all the aformentioned features
B. SURVEY & EXPERIENCE / BOARDS: 
–Updated Drawings 1 & 2 + Images 1 & 2
C. DESIGN / BOARDS: 
After having identified a problematic, the Design aims at expressing and mapping 
out the potential solution/s: a project. 
–Drawings 3 & 4: A0 Drafts your design within the city (line drawing) in two 
complementary views (plan, section, axo, details etc)
–Images 3 & 4: A0 Montages of your design within the city, depicting two 
complementary situations

ARSENAL GUIDELINES
The Arsenal is an online platform: a collection of curated and instrumentalised media. 
Within the design studio and beyond, it serves as a presentation tool of the student 
projects (cases). As it used both for storage and production, the Arsenal is distinguished 
from an archive: content or resources become available assets, ready-to-use in a spar – not 
unlike its historical warfare namesake. 
LOGIN
Group username & password: received by e-mail
PROCESSING
– Before uploading any media, check if it already exists on the Arsenal.
– Please process the content with great care: double check for typos, blanks, gaps, etc. 
It is a shared and public platform – responsible intendance is the best way of showing 
respect for your own work and that of others.
NAMING – AUTHOR
[surname, firstname] Clinton, Bill
[surname 1, firstname 1; surname 2, firstname 2] Clinton, Bill; Clooney, George
If the name is an establishment or institution, put only the name of this establishment / 
institution. [NASA]
NAMING – TITLE
Never put the name of your Agent as title.
Titling the media correctly: if the title is too long, shorten it accordingly – please be 
reasonable and coherent here.
NAMING – TAGS
No need to tag your media with your agent as this is already included within the upload 
metadata. Tag smartly: rather none than a bad one.
RAW / PROCESSED
Check RAW whenever the media is unmodified. 
Check PROCESSED whenever you are the original author or have modified the media.
QUOTES
Delete all formatting.
No quotation marks.
No hyphenation.
IMAGES
High-res only.
Optimise the image in Photoshop before uploading (Saturation, Contrast, etc.)
