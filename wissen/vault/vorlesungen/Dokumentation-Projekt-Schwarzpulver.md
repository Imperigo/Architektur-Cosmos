# Dokumentation Projekt Schwarzpulver

## S. 2

He grew up in a village facing the Taiwan straits. As a result the cultural revolution 
in China brought gunpowder and its overwhelming destructive power very close to 
Cai. 
He followed his father’s footsteps in becoming a painter and calligrapher. Unlike his 
father, he felt driven to challenge conventions to find his own expression. This desire 
led Cai to gunpowder. Although naturally cautious, by choosing gunpowder as a material, he relinquishes control over the artwork, freeing himself from social conventions and restraints.
„It purges violence and destruction from this society and era into something beautiful. It’s another type of cure for my childhood.“ He sees Construction and destruction 
as a circle of life. 
1999 won the golden Lion at Venice Biennale, a scene made out of clay statues 
depicting the way a cruel landlord would exploit their peasants. He left them unfired, 
causing them to crack and disintegrate over the course of the biennale. 
From then on he created many explosion events, all over the world. Most notably the 
fireworks for the 2008 Beijing olympics opening ceremony. 
„If you were to ask me what makes my art connect with people I’d say it is my expression of our shared childhood dreams and my expression of pure human emotion.“ 
White tone: Oasis in Queensland. What if it was the last oasis on earth left by nature? The pool is surrounded by 99 animals, some aggressive like tigers, others gentle 
like horses and sheep, all humbly lowering their head and drinking from this pond.
He does not want to make a statement about current society and political events. 
Through other forms of allegory, he indicates something that in the end is still very 
open ended. He uses commentary on social and political topics in a very indirect 
way. 
„Sometimes in life, reality isn’t always ideal but through my art I can find my perfect 
reality.“ 
„Using Explosives to create Beauty, rather than warfare and violence, provides a 
slither of hope for our shared homan future.“

## S. 26

Our newest addition to our fireworks is Blackpow­
der. Which should help us to get closer to the 
Artwork of Cai.
For precaution we used a small dose of Blackpow­
der to get a feeling for it‘s use. As a base to ignite 
the powder, we used a cement board. It breaks 
easier than normal stone and the surface is way 
more editable.
The result was astonishing. For the first time we 
saw a glimpse of the power of Blackpowder. The 
heat created Bubbles and small craters in the ce­
ment. Right in the mix was also traces of soot.

## S. 36

Cai Guo-Qiang uses canvas to create his art with 
black powder. By drawing on it and igniting the 
black powder, he creates his art on a two-dimen­
sional white canvas. We tried to recreate exactly 
this art.
The difference between his art and our experi­
ment is that he was able to draw much more de­
tail than we could. He also used better prepared 
blackpowder. Our blackpowder did not produce 
the explosiveness of his work.
Our mixture also had a lot of lumps and was left 
behind with the soot.
