Policy transfer vs. lesson-drawing
added value of transnational cooperation
Dr. Ana Peric
‚POLICY TRANSFER‘
„Knowledge about policies, administrative arrangements, institutions 
and ideas in one political setting is used in the development of 
policies, administrative arrangements, institutions and ideas in 
another political setting“. 
(Dolowitz & Marsh, 2000)
‚LESSON-DRAWING‘
„Voluntarily engagement of actors in an active search for new ideas 
and partners as response to a problem, but without necessary 
change of the policies and practices“.
(Dühr, Colomb, Nadin, 2010)
Transnational cooperation

Dr. Ana Peric
Europeanisation of (domestic) spatial planning
Europeanisation: a three-step process
1. Construction: ‘planning for Europe’ 
planning at the European scale, strategic planning
2. Diffusion: ‘planning for Europe’ on ‘planning in Europe’
influence of EU policies on planning in nation-states (and vice-versa)
3. Institutionalisation: implementation of EU policies 
transfer the beliefs and norms into stable organisations and procedures
Methodological challenge:
‘conformance’ (thematic correspondence) vs. performance
Europeanisation vs. globalisation

Channels of the Europeanisation of spatial planning
1.
Spatially defined (or targeted) EU sectoral policies with strong 
territorial impact >> certain territorial scope
(Cohesion Policy, Transport Policy)
2.
Non-spatially defined (or targeted) EU sectoral policies with 
strong territorial impact >> entire EU 
(Competition Policy, Common Agricultural Policy, Erasmus) 
3. EU legislation with direct impact on domestic planning legislation
(EU Environmental Policy, EIA, SIA)
4.
Specific initiatives in European spatial planning with impacts on 
planning policies and practice
(ESPON, INTERREG, ESDP, TA)

“Planners should remain humble and patient, as well as ambitious
and committed. On the one hand they should acknowledge that their
power to guide spatial development and to defend greenfield
consumption
against
infrastructural
development
and
property
speculation is quite limited. While on the other hand, they should not
surrender to neo-liberal attitudes and short-term incrementalism. It is
still the task of planners to develop longer-term visions and to seek
spatial justice. Without allies in the ‘real’ world however their visions

influence in society.”
