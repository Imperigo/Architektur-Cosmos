# BaumannAndrin_Initial_Session5_My_Industrial_DataCenter_Triptych

## S. 1

MY INDUSTRIAL DATA CENTER TRIPTYCH
FIELD WORK EXCERCISE
conceived by Flora Mary Bartlett
Name
Andrin Baumann
Zurich
30.10.2025
Winterthur is a city marked and known for its industrial labor, a hotspot of industrial 
culture. Brick upon brick, clinker upon clinker, these are the tactile materials that re­
veal it: the simplicity and rawness of the twentieth-century factory halls. They embody 
repetition and structured form. Window, brick, window, brick, rising ever higher, ending 
in a final crowning line.
The Data Center Winterthur is an adaptation of this repetition into the vertical. A 
tower, not a hall. A tower striving to reach as high as possible. A tower whose antenna 
reaches toward a scale larger than Winterthur itself. Transmission and reception, as if 
on a mountain peak with its aerial. 1 0 1 1 0 0 0, the building communicates with Zu­
rich, Basel, Bern, Germany, Thailand, Japan, Australia. Connected to the entire world. 
Standing in the heart of Winterthur. A code inscribed in its façade, transmitting like 
Morse, the binary pulse of its existence. Hidden behind a house and greenery, only 
the tower rises above, as if seeking to merge with nature.
From another perspective, the tower stands within the infrastructure of the SBB, fully 
exposed to the barren landscape of rails, gravel, and metal. Again repetition of data, 
1 0 1 1 1 0 0 0. The façade now largely glass, as if the building wanted to open itself 
to the outside, to reveal what lies within. Yet inside the tower, all is sealed again. What 
might lie at the very top?
Like a server rack, sober and ordered, a closed façade, reflective glass. From up 
close, it feels intimidating, tall, private, inaccessible. Standing around it, one grows 
uneasy, watched, observed. The tower omnipresent, dominant. Like a church, yet not 
one that tells time, but one that seeks synchronization with other cities. A single win­
dow breaks the rhythm, an anomaly, an error in the matrix, an opening for the over­
heated tower to breathe.
Unlike other data centers, this one stands as an elephant in the room. Visible from ev­
erywhere, a landmark within its field. But the closer one approaches, the more distant 
and inaccessible it becomes. Nothing fenced, yet nothing revealed. It seems to play 
with open cards, but where are the entrances. Only deliveries. For what. More data.
