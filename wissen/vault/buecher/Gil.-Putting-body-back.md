THE EXPOSITION PAVILION: A PLAY OF STRUCTURE AND SIGN |
By locating exposition architecture within a structuralist semiotic framework, the central idea is to show that a semiotic analysis of
expositions addresses issues that might otherwise go unnoticed. This is done firstly by identifying general features of expositions,
secondly by appealing to concepts developed in semiotic analyses that are useful in understanding expositions, and thirdiy by
applying such an analysis to four specific exposition pavilions. The paper contains a discussion of how a semiotic analysis of
expositions raises legitimate questions about exposition architecture in general and whether there really can be a field of study
called architectural semiotics. |

ON ARCHITECTURAL SKETCHING |
Architects often explain their works as craft, as know-how, and as autonomies in sketching, to which other architects respond with
sketches of their own. This paper focuses on the creative abilities of architects, overlooking the knowledge culture of the profession.
“Architectural know-how” can lead to “architectural know-what". It is possible, it is argued, to find architectural knowledge hiding in
single sketches. This helps in understanding connections between general trends and individual works. Architectural knowledge
must not be understood as truth, but as something fabricated. The mystery of the emergence of a single architectural work remains.

1. The Inversion of the Subject

“Indeed, consciousness is defined by intentionality. By intentionality, consciousness transcends itself.” — Jean Paul Sartre’

The problem of Modernism is the location of the subject. The problem of
locating the subject is that subjectivity is an embedded condition of existence
indirectiy known via the characteristics of our mediated sensibilities. The
subject, this essay proposes, having ‘imprisoned itself in empirical consciousness,” is currently an all-pervasive, non-objective mode of awareness
perpetually correlating, extending and withdrawing itself into unique, inverse,
holistic relationships, modulating perception as the primary media, resulting
in no less than the eternally updating manifestation of intention, whether active or otherwise. By contrast, though not in opposition, rational thought sustains external equivalents, thereby eliminating that which we essentially are
not, pointing to the subject as an undisclosed model. Meaningful correspondences intentionally mirror the prevailing anthropological paradigm.

Culturally, psychological paradigms such as structured time, depth of
field and personal property order physical phenomena and coordinate our

essential malleability and therefore dynamic characterization of being. Active consciousness, according to Sartre “one ofthe most difficult problems of

Technology into Abstraction

Abstraction is a characterization and a location, a quantity and a position, a
noun and a dynamic adverb. It is an immaterial entity that adjusts to the
momentum of mind in all of its manifestations. It is the component of mind
that exceeds physical limitations and folds the prevailing unknown into the
knowable.

If we presume by our own logic that our origins were completely nothing
or totally something, our consciousness behaves like a diagonal that stimulates and antagonizes any system into a semblance of order. We cannot
engage the entire system at once. It is only possible for some of our actions
to impact a system at once because each expression has a limited range of
frequencies. Mechanical technologies extend the active component of the
human condition while digital technologies extend physiology and consciousness itself.

Consciousness is regarded as the mark of a rational being, yet there is
nothing lineal or sequential about the total field of awareness that exists in
any moment. Consciousness is not, after all, a linear or verbal process. It
was David Hume who demonstrated that there is no causality indicated in
any sequence, natural or logical. “The sequential is merely additive, not
causative”, wrote Marshall McLuhan, adding that Hume never discovered
“the hidden cause of our Western bias towards sequential logic in the all
pervasive technology of the alphabet”.'® Hume, McLuhan might agree, remained in the temple, vacated the text and became stuck in the process of
writing within a format about that which the process was not.

According to McLuhan, technologies are an extension of our
biointelligent faculties. Technology refers not merely to machines, but to any
external system or device which extends human faculties into a system of
isolated and specialized usefulness. We see ourselves in our technology, but
what human faculty was extended as a logical sequence except for the perception of existence itself as non-contiguous? Logic itself may assume many
forms, but the repeatable effects of linear thought remain the core intentions

of Western society while the remainder was cast into abstraction including
vital aspects of ourselves. Given the principle that allthings exist at all scales
of reality at once, wherein does form or vision inhabit itself? The most recent
stage in the transformation of abstract thought are the new models of Nature
that are no longer visible to the naked eye, yet exist as data made visible
through technological media drawing the mobile, the invisible, and the eternal into our perceptual domain. Given the exposure of the contents of traditionally unknown data and the emergent homogenous field, what then
remains to be abstracted? What then is God and Origin? Where is meaning
to be located?
